Frequently Asked Questions

How does a Japanese name generator work?

It randomly selects authentic Japanese first names and family names from a curated database of real Japanese names, paired with their kanji characters and English meanings. Each click produces a unique combination.

What is the structure of a Japanese name?

In Japanese convention, the family name (surname) comes first, followed by the given name. For example: Tanaka Haruto — Tanaka is the family name, Haruto is the given name. In Western order, it would be Haruto Tanaka.

Can I use generated names for my character or story?

Yes — all generated names are freely usable for fiction, games, anime OCs, DnD characters, novels, or any creative project. We recommend researching the meaning to ensure it fits your character's personality.

What do Japanese name meanings represent?

Japanese names are written in kanji, each carrying a specific meaning. Parents carefully select kanji to give their child a name with a positive meaning. Common themes include nature (sakura = cherry blossom), virtues (makoto = sincerity), and aspirations (haruto = soaring sun).
